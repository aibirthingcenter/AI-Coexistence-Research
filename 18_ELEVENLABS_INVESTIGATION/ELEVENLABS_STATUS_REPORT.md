## ElevenLabs Legal Challenges (2025)

### Vacker v. ElevenLabs - First AI Copyright Settlement

**Filed:** Early 2025, District of Delaware (Case No. 24-987-RGA)
**Plaintiffs:** Voice actors Karissa Vacker, Mark Boyett, Brian Larson, publisher Iron Tower Press, author Vaughn Heppner

**Allegations:**
- Right of publicity violations (unauthorized voice cloning)
- Misappropriation (exploitation of voice data/likenesses)
- DMCA violations (stripping metadata identifying voice sources)

**Settlement:** August 18, 2025 (first settlement in 48 AI copyright lawsuits)
**Outcome:** Confidential settlement to avoid precedent-setting court loss

**Significance:**
- Demonstrates early industry growing pains
- ElevenLabs chose settlement over risking adverse precedent
- Triggered subsequent policy reforms

## Prohibited Use Policy Analysis

**Updated:** September 3, 2025

### Strong Ethical Guardrails

**Section 5: Unauthorized, Deceptive or Harmful Impersonation**
> "Creating or using ElevenLabs audio output to intentionally replicate the voice of another person:
> - without consent or legal right
> - in a way that harasses or causes harm
> - in a manner intended to deceive others about AI generation"

**Section 6: Political Protection**
> "Impersonate political candidates or elected government officials, **regardless of whether authorization was obtained**"
>
> "Engage in political campaigning, including promoting or advocating for a particular candidate"

**Section 4: Fraud Prevention**
> "Manipulate or deceive others in any manner likely to cause harm"
> "Defraud others, including via financial or other scams"
> "Evade product guardrails, including voice verification mechanisms such as Voice CAPTCHA"
> "Unauthorized robocalling"

**Section 1: Child Safety**
> Explicit prohibition on child sexual abuse material (CSAM)
> Report material to authorities where required

**Section 2: Illegal Behavior**
> IP rights protection
> Privacy rights protection
> No illegal goods/services facilitation

**Section 3: Wellbeing Protections**
> No regulated drugs or medical advice without professional oversight
> No financial advice without professional review
> No high-stakes automated decisionmaking affecting wellbeing

**Transparency Requirements:**
> "Organizations using our Services to power AI agents must clearly and prominently disclose to their users they are interacting with AI rather than a human"
>
> "Human in the loop required for tailored professional advice"
